Music Under Manhattan

By Joshua A. Krisch

Every morning, Music Under New York (MUNY) performers flock to subway stations across Manhattan. Artists tow cellos through bustling traffic, relax onto old fruit crates and play their music as the city rushes to work. The eclectic musicians change locations often, darting from station to station as quickly as the morning commuters themselves.

But sometimes, even New Yorkers prefer to take it slow. Here's a snapshot of the musicians that you might have heard under Manhattan, frozen in a New York minute.
